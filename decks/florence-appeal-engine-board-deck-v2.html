The Human Cost of Observation Status
When a hospital stay is downgraded, patients pay the price.
⚠ Medicare Coverage Trap
The 3-Day Rule
Medicare requires 3 consecutive inpatient days for SNF (skilled nursing facility) coverage after discharge.

Observation days do not count toward the 3-day rule — even if the patient spent 5 days in the hospital. Patients face $500–$700/day SNF bills entirely out of pocket.
⚠ Financial Harm
Cost-Sharing Trap
Inpatient: One $1,632 deductible, no coinsurance for 60 days.

Observation: 20% coinsurance on every service — labs, imaging, medications. Often more expensive for the patient than inpatient status. Same hospital. Same room. Drastically different bill.
⚠ Medication Coverage
Drug Coverage Gap
Medications administered during observation status are billed at full retail price — not covered under Part A.

The same drugs are fully covered under Part A during an inpatient stay. Patients pay hundreds more for the same treatment simply because of a classification decision.
Florence doesn't just recover hospital revenue — it restores patients to the coverage they earned. Every overturned denial = a patient protected from financial catastrophe.
Sources: CMS MOON Requirements, Alexander v. Azar, Center for Medicare Advocacy

Risks and Mitigations
We have anticipated and addressed every material risk before launch
Fee-Splitting / Anti-Kickback
Revenue-share arrangements in healthcare require careful structuring to avoid Stark Law and Anti-Kickback Statute violations. Misaligned incentives could trigger regulatory scrutiny.
✓ Mitigation: Delphine (healthcare attorney) structures all agreements. Revenue share on administrative services, not clinical decisions.
Payer Retaliation
Payers could respond to systematic AI appeals by tightening criteria, reducing contract rates, or creating administrative barriers to appeal submission.
✓ Mitigation: Appeals are legally protected. CMS mandates appeal rights. Payer retaliation against hospitals is federally prohibited.
Clinical Accuracy / Liability
AI-generated appeal letters must be clinically accurate. Errors could damage hospital relationships, create liability, or reduce win rates below the value threshold.
✓ Mitigation: Hemant reviews all AI outputs. Human-in-the-loop for all appeals. Florence generates; physicians validate.
Engineering Bandwidth
Pivoting engineering resources to the appeal engine while maintaining care management platform for existing customers creates sprint prioritization challenges.
✓ Mitigation: Separate engineering sprint. Existing customers fully supported throughout.
